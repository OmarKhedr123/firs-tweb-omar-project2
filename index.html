History of Mukbangs

This is a website about the history of mukbangs.


What is a Mukbang?

A mukbang is a video that has someone (usually the host) eating a large quantity of food with their audience. And the quantities of food got
progressively larger over time, leading to more extravagant and visually "appealing" presentations.



How it started out

Mukbangs started out as a South Korean live stream around 2009 and was live-streaming on plat forms like AfreecaTV, as a cultural response to
loneliness and changing eating habits in South Korea.


How it rose to fame

Mukbangs gained popularity through social media influencers like Tzuyang, Hamzy, Nikocado Avocado, Bloveslife, and Trisha Paytas , on YouTube,
where creators could reach a global audience. The combination of food, entertainment, and interaction made mukbangs a unique form of content that
appealed to many viewers.
